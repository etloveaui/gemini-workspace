The 10-year Treasury yield closed at 4.26% on Friday, declining 7.1 basis points to reach its lowest level since August 13 following Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell's Jackson Hole speech 4. The bond market rallied significantly, with the iShares Core U.S. Aggregate Bond ETF (AGG) climbing 0.6%, marking its largest daily gain since early June 4.

Current Yield Curve Structure

The Treasury yield curve exhibits a slight inversion in the front end, with short-term rates elevated above intermediate maturities. The policy-sensitive 2-year Treasury note slid 10.2 basis points to 3.689%, also reaching its lowest level since August 13 4.

Powell's Jackson Hole Impact

Powell delivered what State Street's Michael Arone characterized as a "virtuoso performance" by maintaining flexibility in monetary policy while balancing competing risks 4. The Fed Chair acknowledged that "risks to inflation are tilted to the upside, and risks to employment to the downside," while noting the policy rate is now 100 basis points closer to neutral than a year ago, allowing for careful consideration of policy changes 4.

Rate Cut Expectations

Market participants are positioning for the Fed's interest-rate-cutting cycle to resume in September based on the Treasury market's reaction 4. Arone anticipates three rate cuts of 25 basis points each at the Fed's remaining three meetings in 2025 4. Richard Clarida, Pimco's global economic advisor and former Fed vice chair, expects 50 basis points of cuts in both 2025 and 2026 4.

Investment Strategy Considerations

Arone favors the "belly" of the yield curve, specifically intermediate maturities of five to seven years, due to potential volatility in long-term rates stemming from U.S. deficit concerns and global trade system resetting 4. This positioning reflects expectations that longer-duration bonds may face headwinds despite the current rally.
