Sector Performance Analysis

Technology and Communication Services continue driving market gains with double-digit YTD returns of 12.86% and 14.98% respectively, supported by AI infrastructure buildout and cloud expansion 2. Industrials leads all sectors YTD at 16.23%, benefiting from defense spending and capital expenditure cycles. Utilities posted a surprising 13.67% YTD gain as power demand surged from data centers and renewable energy transition accelerated.

Healthcare significantly underperformed with just 0.44% YTD gains as drug pricing pressures and regulatory headwinds weighed on the sector despite innovation in biotech. Consumer Discretionary's 3.90% YTD return reflects mixed consumer sentiment, though today's 3.04% pop suggests renewed confidence. Energy remains the laggard at 2.90% YTD despite recent oil price recovery.

Key Sector Rotation Themes

The dominant rotation theme centers on AI beneficiaries and infrastructure plays, with Technology ETFs commanding $788B in AUM and the lowest 0.27% expense ratios 1. Investors increasingly favor large-cap exposure over small-caps, with U.S. large-blend ETFs attracting $68B in summer flows while small-cap ETFs saw $9B outflows 2.

Sector ETFs captured nearly 20% of equity flows this summer, well above the 6% YTD average, indicating tactical positioning. Technology led sector inflows at $5.8B, followed by Industrials at $3.0B and Communications at $2.4B, while Healthcare faced $2.6B outflows and Energy saw $0.9B exits 2. International allocation increased sharply, with non-U.S. ETFs drawing nearly one-third of total equity flows from June through August, up from just 7% in the prior year period.

Active ETF adoption accelerated with nearly 90% of 2025 launches being active strategies, as active equity ETFs pulled $153B YTD and are on pace to exceed 2024's record $182B 2. The rotation toward quality growth at reasonable valuations persists, with investors seeking exposure to secular themes like AI, defense modernization, and energy transition while avoiding cyclically-sensitive consumer and energy plays.
