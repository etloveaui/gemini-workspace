AAPL gained 1.27% despite YTD losses of 9.05%, driven by reports that Apple is in early discussions with Google to use Gemini AI models for an overhauled Siri version, signaling a potential shift from internal AI development due to engineering setbacks that delayed the Siri upgrade by a year. Loop Capital lowered its price target to $226 from $230 due to mix shifts toward Pro/Pro Max models and lower Air model sales, while the company raised Apple TV+ pricing by 30% to $13 monthly and is manufacturing all four iPhone 17 models in India to reduce China reliance.1318

MSFT rose 0.59% with strong YTD gains of 20.34%, supported by its dominant position as a critical AI infrastructure provider with Azure generating $75B in revenue in fiscal 2025, up 34% YoY, and the company planning nearly $100B in CapEx for fiscal 2026. Microsoft announced a multiyear NFL partnership extension integrating Copilot and Azure AI, while limiting Chinese companies' access to cybersecurity vulnerability notifications after investigating potential leaks, reinforcing its strategic positioning in the AI ecosystem.1325

NVDA advanced 1.72% with YTD gains of 32.54%, though facing headwinds from ceasing H20 AI chip production for China after Beijing urged local firms to avoid the chips due to alleged security concerns, potentially impacting the 13% of revenue from China. Wedbush raised its price target to $210 from $175, citing robust demand and expectations for GB300 server shipments in late Q3 or Q4, with the AI Revolution still in early stages and demand at a 10:1 ratio to supply ahead of next week's earnings.131429

AMZN surged 3.10% despite modest YTD gains of 4.31%, recovering from Q2 tariff concerns while AWS faces scrutiny over whether capacity constraints are limiting its AI market share compared to Azure and Google Cloud, despite 17.5% YoY revenue growth with unclear guidance on when expansion accelerates further.24

GOOGL jumped 3.17% with YTD gains of 8.87%, driven by securing a $10B+ six-year cloud services deal with Meta focused on AI infrastructure and reports of early discussions with Apple to power Siri with Gemini AI models, validating Google Cloud's competitive positioning with $13.6B in Q2 cloud revenue growing 32% and over $50B annual run rate. The U.S. General Services Administration also announced an agreement for Google to provide AI and cloud services across government agencies.69

META gained 2.12% with YTD returns of 28.91%, boosted by the $10B+ Google cloud contract and strong fundamentals including 19.37% revenue growth, 28% EBITDA growth, and 41% bottom-line growth YoY, with net income reaching $18.33B in Q2 2025 versus $13.46B prior year. The company is preparing to unveil "Hypernova" smart glasses with display for $800 in September, while Reality Labs revenue tripled YoY with 2M Ray-Ban smart glasses sold since 2023, though management signaled $68B in 2025 CapEx with continued 2026 investment.721

AVGO rose 1.52% with YTD gains of 26.81%, benefiting from the broader AI infrastructure buildout and semiconductor demand, though specific catalysts were limited compared to other mega-cap tech names.24

CRWD advanced 1.57% with YTD gains of 22.91% despite a challenging month down 10.75%, as the cybersecurity leader continues recovering from earlier operational issues while maintaining its market position in enterprise security solutions.

NFLX declined 0.13% despite strong YTD gains of 35.15%, with options volume above average at 113k contracts and calls leading puts at a 0.79 put/call ratio, suggesting modestly bullish sentiment despite the slight daily decline as content consumption remains sticky.16

NOW gained 1.12% but remains down 16.35% YTD, driven by strong Q2 results and raised 2025 guidance from accelerating AI adoption, with current Remaining Performance Obligations reaching $10.92B growing 24.5% YoY and 89 new deals over $1M in net new ACV, while Pro Plus subscriptions quadrupled and the company targets $1B in AI product Annual Contract Value by 2026.5

TSLA surged 6.22% despite YTD losses of 15.81%, with CEO Elon Musk stating the new Model Y variant won't start US production until end of next year and might never given self-driving advent in America, while William Blair noted the robotaxi service in Austin was half the price of Uber with more luxurious service quality.13

PLTR rose 1.64% with exceptional YTD gains of 109.89%, though trading at a forward P/E of 278 making it one of the most expensive US companies, with Wedbush believing the company will grow into its valuation and could reach trillion-dollar market cap within 2-3 years due to AI capabilities, despite concerns about unsustainable valuation multiples driven by AI hype.2229
