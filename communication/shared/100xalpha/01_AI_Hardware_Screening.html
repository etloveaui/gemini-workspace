Market Insight: AI Hardware Market Experiencing Unprecedented Growth Amid Supply Chain Constraints and Sovereign AI Demand

The AI hardware market is experiencing explosive growth with unprecedented demand across all segments, driven by the rapid adoption of generative AI, reasoning models, and sovereign AI initiatives. The global AI hardware market is projected to reach $210.50B by 2034, representing a CAGR of 22.43% from 2025 to 2034 [1] . Citi forecasts AI revenues to reach $780B in 2030 from $43B in 2025, representing an almost 80% compound annual growth rate [2] . The AI accelerator market alone is projected to grow from $126B in CY24 to $400B in CY27, $500B in CY28, and $650B in CY30, representing a 26% CAGR [3] .

Market Performance and Current Conditions

The AI hardware sector has entered a period of extraordinary expansion, with hyperscaler capital expenditures driving unprecedented infrastructure investment. Hyperscalers collectively plan approximately $320B of AI-specific CAPEX in CY25, roughly 30% higher than 2024 [2] . Individual hyperscaler 2025 capex estimates include Amazon ~$103.3B (up 24% Y/Y), Alphabet ~$73.0B (up 39% Y/Y), Meta ~$65.8B (up 77% Y/Y), Microsoft ~$78.9B (up 42% Y/Y), and Oracle ~$26.1B (up 143% Y/Y) [4] . Morgan Stanley estimates a total global data center spend of $2.9 trillion through year-end 2028, excluding power investments, with 85% allocated to AI-specific data centers [5] .

Leading semiconductor companies are reporting exceptional growth driven by AI demand. TSMC's net revenue for the six months ended June 30, 2025, was $1,773,045,533 thousand, a significant increase from $1,266,154,378 thousand for the same period in 2024 [6] . NVIDIA's revenue for Q1 FY2026 was $44,062 million, a 69% increase year-over-year, with Data Center revenue of $39,112 million, up 73% year-over-year [7] . AMD reported $7.685 billion in net revenue for Q2 2025, a 32% increase year-over-year, though the company recorded approximately $800 million in inventory charges related to export controls on MI308 products [8] .

Sector Performance and Key Catalysts

Product Ramps and Technology Transitions

The market is experiencing significant momentum from next-generation product launches. NVIDIA's Blackwell GPUs are projected to reach 3.6 million units in 2025, a substantial increase from 1.3 million Hopper GPUs in 2024 [9] . Major hyperscalers are deploying nearly 1,000 NVL 72 racks (72,000 Blackwell GPUs) per week [10] . AMD's MI350 series entered mass production in June 2025, offering up to a 4x generational leap in AI compute for both training and inference [11] . The data center AI accelerator TAM is expected to grow more than 60% annually to $500 billion in 2028 [11] .

Sovereign AI Emergence

Sovereign AI has emerged as a critical growth driver, with announced government commitments totaling over $1.6T over a multi-year period [2] . The US's Stargate project alone is projected to cost up to $500B between 2025-2029, while other major commitments include the European Union ($289B), China ($232B), South Korea ($129B), and Japan ($135B) [2] . NVIDIA estimates the sovereign AI market could approach $1 trillion over several years, with countries needing their own AI capabilities to maintain control over data, language, and culture [12] .

Hyperscaler CAPEX Acceleration

Infrastructure spending is expected to surpass $400B in CAPEX by 2025, easing capacity constraints [2] . Microsoft expects Q1 FY2026 capital expenditures to be over $30 billion, driven by continued strong demand signals [13] . Oracle's CapEx for FY2026 is projected to be over $25 billion to meet demand for data center capacity [14] . Amazon's purchases of property and equipment were $31.4 billion in Q2 2025, compared to $16.4 billion in Q2 2024 [15] .

Major Risks and Supply Chain Challenges

Export Controls and Geopolitical Tensions

Export controls represent the most significant near-term risk to market growth. The US government issued new export controls on NVIDIA's H20 chips in April 2025, resulting in a $4.5 billion charge and the inability to ship $2.5 billion in H20 revenue in Q1 [7] . AMD recorded approximately $800 million in inventory and related charges associated with export controls on MI308 products [8] . However, recent developments show AMD and NVIDIA have received initial export licenses for AI chips to China, with both companies agreeing to pay the US government 15% of their revenue from Chinese AI chip sales [16] .

Supply Chain Bottlenecks

Supply-chain bottlenecks remain a significant constraint, particularly due to TSMC's 3nm EUV constraints and HBM3e shortages, which dictate chip launch timelines more than design readiness [2] . The pushout of GB200 rack mass production is primarily a supply issue, with component suppliers facing yield rate issues [9] . UBS forecasts total HBM demand to reach 21.3B Gb in 2025E, up 119% YoY, with HBM accounting for an estimated 32% of total DRAM industry revenues in 2026E [17] . Scaling high-speed interconnects faces supply chain constraints, with demand for 800G and 1.6T optics outpacing readiness [2] .

Key Catalysts and Risk Assessment

Company-Specific Catalysts

• NVIDIA Blackwell Ramp: Blackwell contributed nearly 70% of data center compute revenue in Q1 2026, with the transition from Hopper nearly complete [27] . Major hyperscalers are deploying nearly 1,000 NVL 72 racks (72,000 Blackwell GPUs) per week, with Microsoft already deploying tens of thousands of Blackwell GPUs and ramping to hundreds of thousands of GB200s [27] . Blackwell Ultra (GB300) sampling began in May 2025 with production shipments expected in Q2, delivering 1.5x more AI performance than GB200 [27] [28] .

• TSMC CoWoS Capacity Expansion: TSMC is aggressively expanding CoWoS capacity from 35-40kwpm at end-2024 to 80kwpm by end-2025, with plans to reach 120-130kwpm by end-2026 [29] . Monthly CoWoS capacity is projected to reach 65,000-75,000 units by end-2025, rising to around 100,000 units by end-2026 [30] . Despite this expansion, demand continues to outstrip supply with NVIDIA accounting for over 60% of total CoWoS demand [31] .

• Micron HBM Growth: HBM revenue grew nearly 50% sequentially in fiscal Q3 2025, with the company expecting to reach HBM market share similar to its overall DRAM share (20-25%) in the second half of calendar 2025 [32] . HBM4 volume production is expected in calendar 2026, offering over 60% bandwidth increase compared to HBM3E with 20% lower power consumption [32] . Micron's HBM is sold out for 2025, with strong demand visibility for 2026 supply [32] .

• AMD Export Risk: AMD recorded approximately $800 million in inventory and related charges associated with export controls on MI308 products [8] . However, AMD has secured initial export licenses for AI chips to China, with the U.S. government receiving a 15% cut of AI chip sales revenue from China [33] . The MI350 series entered mass production in June 2025, offering up to a 4x generational leap in AI compute [11] .

Macro and Sector Risks

• Tariff Implementation: President Trump announced a 100% tariff on semiconductor chips manufactured overseas unless companies have committed to building in the U.S. [34] . TSMC is expected to be exempt due to its $165B investment in Arizona operations [35] . In a dire tariff scenario, BofA estimates total semiconductor industry sales impact of 8-11% in 2025 and 10-14% in 2026 [36] .

• Rare Earth Supply Disruptions: China controls 65% of rare earth mining and 88% of refining, with recent export controls on medium and heavy rare earths including terbium, dysprosium, and neodymium-iron-boron permanent magnets [37] . Global rare earth magnet exports to the U.S. declined 93.3% year-on-year in May 2025 [38] . Demand for NdPr is projected to double by 2035, with robotics contributing significantly to growth [38] .

• Energy Consumption Constraints: AI factory deployments are accelerating with nearly 100 NVIDIA-powered AI factories deployed in Q1 2026, with the average number of GPUs powering each factory doubling [39] . Proposed 100% tariffs on semiconductors could increase AI server costs by up to 75%, potentially pricing smaller firms out of frontier AI development and resulting in 15-20 fewer hyperscale facilities [40] . Hyperscale companies plan to spend over $350B on AI-related data centers in 2025 alone [40] .
