Executive Summary and Market Landscape

The energy sector is experiencing unprecedented transformation driven by AI datacenter demand, with US electricity consumption projected to grow 2.4% CAGR through 2030 compared to flat growth over the past decade [1] . Goldman Sachs forecasts global datacenter power demand to increase 160%-165% by 2030 versus 2023 levels, with AI workloads consuming 10-20x more power than conventional CPU-based cloud workloads [2] . Major hyperscalers including Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, and Meta are expected to spend $1.8 trillion combined over 2024-2028, primarily dedicated to AI and datacenter infrastructure [3] . US datacenter peak utility power is estimated to reach 89.0 GW by 2030, up from 51.7 GW in 2025, representing an 11% CAGR [4] . The confluence of rising power demand, historical underinvestment in infrastructure, and extreme weather events is driving significant investment tailwinds across the energy value chain [5] . Bull case: Hyperscalers demonstrate willingness to pay Green Reliability Premiums representing only 2%-3% of EBITDA for reliable power sourcing [5] . Bear case: Grid connection times for datacenters have increased from 2-3 years to 5-10 years due to significant transmission backlogs, creating critical bottlenecks [6] .

Market Landscape Analysis

Oil & Gas: Natural gas revenues surged 98% in Q2 2025 for EOG Resources driven by 66% higher composite prices and 19% increased deliveries [7] . Goldman Sachs projects 3.3 bcf/d incremental natural gas demand by 2030 from datacenter-driven power generation, representing roughly 10% increase in gas consumed in the power market [8] . Datacenter developers are increasingly interested in natural gas due to reliability against grid instability, with studies suggesting datacenter power demand could require nearly 2 bcf/d of natural gas if fully serviced by gas-fired generation [9] . Expanding margins: Natural gas producers benefiting from higher realized prices and increased demand from power generation. Compressing margins: Oil segment facing headwinds with crude oil revenues declining 19% in Q2 2025 due to lower composite prices [7] .

LNG: LNG Canada train 1 operated at 50% capacity utilization in July-August 2025 despite technical issues, with train 2 projected to begin producing in early 2026 [10] . European LNG imports forecasted to increase to 9.6 bcm in August 2025 from 7.3 bcm in August 2024 [11] . BofA raised LNG price objectives citing improving outlook for 2H25, with transatlantic arbitrage moving to $8.7/mmbtu for 2H25 [12] . Expanding margins: Strong demand growth and improving price realizations supporting LNG project economics. Compressing margins: Potential oversupply concerns with 174 MTPA capacity under construction globally expected online 2025-2028 [13] .

Renewables: US electricity demand growing 2%-3% annually with regions like Texas and Mid-Atlantic seeing 10%+ growth from datacenters [14] . Developers plan to add 64 GW of new capacity in 2025, with over half being solar [14] . NextEra Energy originated 3.2 GW of new projects since last earnings call, including over 1 GW serving hyperscalers for AI buildout [15] . Expanding margins: Utility-scale solar costs $0.04/kWh, less than half the cost of new coal or gas peaker plants [14] . Compressing margins: Supply chain constraints and higher interest rates impacting project economics, with Green Energy sector median EV/Revenue multiple declining to 5.7x in Q4 2024 from 11.1x peak in Q4 2020 [16] .

Storage: Goldman Sachs expects battery storage to comprise 15% of incremental generation capacity through 2030 to support datacenter demand [1] . Global storage deployments anticipated to grow 35% year-over-year in 2025, with US utility-scale storage up mid-teens [17] . Expanding margins: Critical role in grid stability and renewable integration driving strong demand growth. Compressing margins: Technology cost deflation and increased competition pressuring project returns.

Nuclear: Major tech companies exploring nuclear solutions with Microsoft, Amazon, and Google investing in large-scale nuclear and small modular reactors [2] . Growing AI power demand seen as main catalyst for first expansion of US nuclear capacity in a generation [18] . Expanding margins: Long-term contracts and capacity premiums supporting project economics. Compressing margins: Extended development timelines and regulatory hurdles limiting near-term deployment.

Grid Infrastructure: Over $700 billion in grid investment anticipated through 2030 in the US, with transmission spend growing faster than distribution to support datacenter buildouts [1] . Edison Electric Institute reported record $178 billion utility industry investment in 2024, with capital expenditure projected to exceed $1.1 trillion over next five years [14] . Expanding margins: Regulated utility model providing stable returns on massive infrastructure investments. Compressing margins: Cost inflation and supply chain constraints pressuring project economics and timelines.

Screening Methodology and Top Companies Analysis

Our proprietary screening methodology employs a multi-factor quantitative framework to identify US-listed energy companies positioned to capitalize on AI-driven datacenter demand and energy infrastructure growth. The screening universe includes companies with float-adjusted market capitalizations ≥$5.0B and 30-day average daily volume ≥$50.0M, ensuring adequate liquidity for institutional investment [19] .

Factor Set and Weighting Structure: The model incorporates seven weighted factors: Revenue Growth (20%), Free Cash Flow Yield (25%), Rule of 40 efficiency metric (15%), Return on Invested Capital (15%), WACC-ROIC Spread (15%), and EPS Revision momentum (10%). An additional AI/Energy Exposure factor (weighted at 25% for qualifying companies) captures material exposure to datacenter power demand, natural gas infrastructure serving power generation, and energy equipment serving AI buildout [5] [1] [2] .

Normalization and Proxies: Financial metrics are normalized using z-scores across the energy sector universe, with outliers winsorized at the 5th and 95th percentiles. AI exposure is quantified through revenue attribution to datacenter infrastructure solutions, power generation equipment for hyperscalers, and natural gas pipeline capacity serving power markets experiencing datacenter-driven demand growth [20] [21] [22] . Companies reporting >10% revenue exposure to AI-related infrastructure or serving regions with >5% projected datacenter load growth qualify for the AI exposure premium [4] [23] .

Key Catalysts & Risks

Catalysts:
• LNG Export Growth: US LNG export capacity expansion driving 3.3 bcf/d incremental natural gas demand by 2030, with LNG Canada train 1 operating at 50% capacity utilization despite technical issues and train 2 projected to begin producing in early 2026 [10] . European LNG imports forecasted to increase to 9.6 bcm in August 2025 from 7.3 bcm in August 2024 [11]

• Renewable Buildouts: Developers plan to add 64 GW of new capacity in 2025, with over half being solar, while NextEra Energy originated 3.2 GW of new projects since last earnings call, including over 1 GW serving hyperscalers for AI buildout [15] . US electricity demand growing 2%-3% annually with regions like Texas and Mid-Atlantic seeing 10%+ growth from datacenters [14]

• Nuclear SMR Approvals: NuScale secured early NRC Standard Design Approval for its uprated 77MWe SMR in 2Q25, strengthening regulatory position and expanding beyond prior 50MWe design [25] . Major tech companies exploring nuclear solutions with Microsoft, Amazon, and Google investing in large-scale nuclear and small modular reactors [2]

• Grid Investments: Over $700 billion in grid investment anticipated through 2030 in the US, with transmission spend growing faster than distribution to support datacenter buildouts [1] . Edison Electric Institute reported record $178 billion utility industry investment in 2024 [14]

• AI Datacenter Contracts: Several data center companies announced partnerships with nuclear companies for power capacity, including Amazon with Talen Energy (960 MW), Microsoft with Constellation Energy (835 MW), and Amazon with X-energy (5,000 MW) [26]

Risks:
• Oil Price Volatility: Goldman Sachs expects Brent crude prices to fall to $56 a barrel in 2026, with companies needing oil prices of $64 to be profitable and $83 for drilling increases according to Kansas City Fed Survey [27] . BofA projects Brent crude oil to average $65/bbl and WTI to average $61/bbl in 2025 [28]

• Carbon Regulation: The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) will require importers in six industries to purchase certificates covering at least 80% of their emissions starting January 2026 [29] . Environmental markets proliferating globally with 38 countries and 13 states having emissions trading systems [30]

• Cost Overruns: Rapid increase in steel prices in the US due to tariffs negatively impacting margins for oil producers, with marginal production costs increased and wellhead oil breakevens for most basins ranging from $50 to $60/bbl of WTI [10] . Supply chain constraints and higher interest rates impacting project economics across renewable development [16]
