Executive Summary and Market Landscape

• Infrastructure spending surge: Hyperscaler capital expenditures are projected to reach $347.2 billion in 2025, representing a 45% year-over-year increase, with data center capex alone estimated at $280.8 billion [1] . Microsoft leads with $78.9 billion, followed by Amazon at $103.3 billion and Alphabet at $73.0 billion [1] .

• AI compute demand acceleration: The data center AI accelerator TAM is expected to exceed $500 billion by 2028, with inference workloads growing over 80% annually and becoming the largest driver of AI compute demand [2] . NVIDIA's data center revenue reached $39 billion in Q1 FY26, up 73% year-over-year [3] .

• Supply chain constraints persist: Leading-edge DRAM supply remains tight due to robust HBM demand, with Micron's HBM business now exceeding $6 billion run rate [4] . TSMC's advanced packaging capacity is doubling in 2025 to support AI accelerator demand, yet customer demand still far exceeds supply ability [5] .

• Margin compression across infrastructure: Microsoft Cloud gross margin decreased to 68%, down two points year-over-year from AI infrastructure scaling [6] . Google Cloud achieved 20.7% operating margin while investing heavily in technical infrastructure [7] . NVIDIA expects gross margins to moderate to low-70s as Blackwell ramps [8] .

• Enterprise adoption inflection: AI comprises roughly 10-15% of IT budgets in 2025, with 70% of customers accelerating AI initiatives over the past six months [9] . Goldman Sachs projects Gen-AI allocation to increase from 5% to 13% of IT budgets within three years [10] .

• Sovereign AI emergence: Countries are investing over $1.6 trillion in AI initiatives, with sovereign AI representing a potential $1 trillion market opportunity over several years [11] [12] . This includes significant commitments from Middle Eastern nations and European sovereign cloud initiatives.

The AI infrastructure landscape is experiencing unprecedented capital deployment driven by three compounding factors: hyperscaler capacity expansion, enterprise AI adoption acceleration, and sovereign computing initiatives. Supply chains face acute constraints in advanced semiconductors and high-bandwidth memory, while margin dynamics reflect the transition from software-centric to infrastructure-intensive business models. The sector exhibits expanding margins in AI-native applications offset by compressing margins in traditional cloud services as providers scale AI infrastructure. Advanced packaging and networking represent the fastest-growing sub-segments, with TSMC's CoWoS capacity doubling and AI networking revenue projected to exceed $6 billion in 2025 [13] .

Screening Methodology and Top 15 Results

Our screening methodology for US-listed AI infrastructure companies employs a multi-factor quantitative framework designed to identify companies with superior growth profiles, operational efficiency, and market positioning within the AI value chain. The methodology incorporates both fundamental financial metrics and AI-specific performance indicators to capture the unique dynamics of this rapidly evolving sector.

• Market Capitalization and Liquidity Filters: Minimum market capitalization of $5 billion and average daily trading volume of at least $25 million over the trailing 12-month period to ensure institutional-grade liquidity [14]

• AI Infrastructure Classification: Companies must demonstrate direct exposure to AI infrastructure through semiconductor manufacturing, data center hardware, networking equipment, memory solutions, or foundational AI software platforms [14] [15]

• Financial Health Metrics: Revenue growth sustainability, free cash flow generation capability, and return on invested capital above sector medians to identify operationally sound enterprises

• Profitability and Efficiency Ratios: Rule of 40 (revenue growth + FCF margin), ROIC-WACC spread analysis, and earnings revision momentum to capture both growth and profitability dynamics [16]

• Normalization Approach: All financial metrics are normalized using z-scores relative to sector peers, with equal weighting applied across growth, profitability, and efficiency factors to prevent bias toward any single metric

Key Market Drivers and Risks

Catalysts

• HBM adoption acceleration: High Bandwidth Memory demand remains exceptionally strong, with Micron's HBM business now exceeding $6 billion run rate [18] . Applied Materials expects revenues from advanced DRAM customers to grow >40% in FY25 as they ramp investments in DDR5 and HBM [33] . KLA's advanced packaging business is projected to exceed $850 million in calendar 2025, driven by increasing HBM chip stacks [22] .

• CoWoS capacity expansion: TSMC's advanced packaging capacity is doubling in 2025 to support AI accelerator demand, yet customer demand still far exceeds supply ability [5] . Applied Materials' packaging business is expected to grow to over $3 billion in the next few years, with HBM packaging tools representing $700 million of their $1.7 billion packaging business [34] .

• REIT leasing momentum: Data centers under construction in the US nearly doubled in 2023 to 5.3 GW, increasing to almost 6.5 GW in 2024 [35] . Over $28 billion in US data center projects entered planning stages in 1H25, with colocation companies raising over $41 billion in equity and debt to support new development [35] . Digital Realty is experiencing significant AI workload contribution, with 50% in Q1 and hyperscalers contributing about 25% in the most recent quarter [36] .

Risks

• Export ban escalation: NVIDIA incurred a $4.5 billion charge in Q1 FY26 for H20 inventory write-downs due to new US export controls, with the $50 billion China market effectively closed to US industry [3] [37] . AMD recorded approximately $800 million in inventory charges for MI308 products due to export restrictions [38] . The White House's "AI Action Plan" includes proposals to strengthen export controls on AI compute chips and develop new controls on semiconductor manufacturing sub-systems [39] .

• Supply bottlenecks intensifying: NVIDIA has experienced extended lead times of more than 12 months and has paid premiums, provided deposits, and entered into long-term supply agreements [37] . ASML notes increasing uncertainty driven by macro-economic and geopolitical developments, with risks including restrictions on shipments under export controls [40] . Applied Materials acknowledges "some impact" from China export restrictions despite business being primarily in mature nodes [41] .

• Cyclical downturn indicators: AMD's risk factors highlight that the semiconductor industry is highly cyclical and has experienced severe downturns [38] . Super Micro's gross margin compressed to 9.5% in Q4 FY25 from 10.2% in Q4 FY24, with margin pressure from tariff impacts and competitive dynamics [42] . Intel faces risks from changes in laws, regulations, or federal administration priorities that could impact CHIPS Act funding [43] .
