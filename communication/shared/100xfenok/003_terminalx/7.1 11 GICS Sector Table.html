Sector Rotation Themes

The current market environment reflects a transition toward a "Recovery" phase, with the US Regime Indicator strengthening in July 2025 based on improvements in EPS revisions, GDP forecasts, and capacity utilization 2. This regime shift historically favors Value, Small Size, Quality, and Low Risk factors while penalizing Momentum and Growth strategies.

Hedge fund positioning data reveals active rotation into Technology (+1.9%), Industrials (+0.6%), and Communication Services (+0.6%) during Q2 2025, while reducing Healthcare (-1.3%), Financials (-0.7%), and Consumer Staples (-0.7%) exposure 4. Despite this tactical shift, institutional investors maintain structural underweights in Technology relative to benchmarks, a trend persisting since 2017 due to megacap concentration concerns.

Valuation Context

Value remains historically cheap relative to Growth, trading at 0.56x forward P/E, nearly two standard deviations below historic averages 2. The Growth vs Value relative forward P/E ratio sits at 1.7x, approaching ex-COVID highs. The S&P 500 Risk Premium of 515 bps as of July 31, 2025 trades slightly above the long-term average of 507 bps, suggesting modest equity attractiveness despite elevated absolute valuations.

Earnings momentum continues supporting equity performance, with S&P 500 EPS growth exceeding 10% in Q2 2025, surpassing both April and January forecasts 5. However, sector dispersion remains pronounced, with seven of eleven sectors experiencing EPS downgrades since January, led by Energy (-15Pp), Materials (-10Pp), and Healthcare (-5Pp) revisions.
