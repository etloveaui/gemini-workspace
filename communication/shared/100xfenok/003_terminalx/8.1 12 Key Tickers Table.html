AAPL declined 0.30% today to $230.89, extending YTD losses to -7.80%. Apple's underperformance stems from mixed iPhone demand dynamics, with Citi noting that while 2Q25 iPhone sales exceeded consensus by 11% due to tariff-related pull-forward demand, the latest iPhone 17 build for 2H25 remains flat YoY at approximately 85M units. The company faces headwinds from Qualcomm's reduced share in upcoming iPhone models, dropping to 70% from 100% in previous launches, while global smartphone shipment growth remains modest at 0.7% for 2025E.7

MSFT fell 0.59% to $517.10 but maintains strong YTD gains of 22.68%. Microsoft's performance is driven by aggressive AI infrastructure investments, with the company targeting $120B in full-year capital expenditures, representing the largest commitment among hyperscalers. The massive capex deployment, funded by strong cash flows rather than debt, positions Microsoft to capitalize on structural shifts in digital services demand and AI adoption across enterprise customers.1

NVDA gained 0.86% to $182.01, delivering 35.54% YTD returns despite recent volatility. The firm increased 2026 estimates to $273.2B revenue and $6.51 EPS, driven by improving supply conditions and sustained hyperscaler AI infrastructure buildouts.19

AMZN edged up 0.20% to $231.49 with modest 5.52% YTD gains. The company's $100B+ annual capex commitment and strong Q2 performance with 13% YoY revenue growth to $167.7B underpin analyst price targets near $265.151
